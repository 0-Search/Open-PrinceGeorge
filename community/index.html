Upcoming public hearings

A public hearing is your formal chance to tell council what you think before they vote — on rezonings and Official Community Plan changes that affect what gets built in your neighbourhood. You don't need to register: you just show up (in person, by Zoom, or by phone) and speak when the Chair calls for the public, or send a written comment beforehand.

Why it matters & how to speak

Council must hold a public hearing before changing zoning or the Official Community Plan. They have to listen to everyone who shows up before they decide — so a few minutes from you genuinely counts, especially since most hearings draw only a handful of people.

  • In person: Council Chambers, 2nd Floor, City Hall, 1100 Patricia Blvd. Be there when the Chair invites public comments.
  • By Zoom: join the meeting (ID 848 2979 7394) and use “Raise Hand” when the Chair calls for the public.
  • By phone: dial 1.778.907.2071, enter the meeting ID, and press *9 to ask to speak.
  • In writing: email cityclerk@princegeorge.ca (or use the City's online form) before 12:00 p.m. on the day of the hearing. Written comments become part of the public record.
  • Time to speak: up to 10 minutes the first time, 5 minutes if you speak again. Give your name and address for the record.

Source: City of Prince George — Participate in Council Meetings ↗

About & method

Open Prince George is an independent project. It is not affiliated with the City of Prince George or any candidate. The aim is simple: make council easy to follow for people who don't have time to follow it.

Every figure links to a public source — council minutes, the city's website, and Open North's Represent data. Found something wrong? Tell us and we'll fix it.
