How to vote in 2026

Everything you need to cast a ballot in the Prince George municipal election on Saturday, October 17, 2026 — in plain language. You'll be voting for one mayor, eight councillors, and School District 57 trustees, all at once.

Can I vote?

You can vote in Prince George if, on voting day, all of these are true:

  • You are 18 or older;
  • You are a Canadian citizen;
  • You have lived in British Columbia for at least 6 months; and
  • You live in Prince George (or you own property here — see below).

You do not need to be on any list ahead of time. British Columbia uses same-day registration: you register right at the voting place when you go to vote (see “What to bring”).

Own property in Prince George but live somewhere else? You may be able to vote as a non-resident property elector. There are extra conditions, so confirm with the City first.

What to bring

Bring two pieces of ID, at least one showing your signature. They need to prove who you are and where you live. Common combinations:

  • A BC driver's licence or BC Services Card (covers both name and address), plus one more piece of ID; or
  • Two of: BC ID card, passport, Status card, credit/debit card, utility bill, bank statement, or a government cheque — as long as together they show your name and address.

Exact accepted-ID lists can change — confirm on the City's elections page before you go.

When & where

  • General voting day: Saturday, October 17, 2026. Polls are typically open 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.
  • Advance voting: the City opens a few advance days in the weeks before. Dates and locations are announced closer to the election.
  • Voting places: assigned by the City and published before the election — you can usually vote at any one in Prince George on general voting day.
  • Can't make it in person? Mail-in ballots are available on request for people who qualify (for example, out of town, or unable to attend). Request early.

Not sure you're eligible, or have questions?

The City of Prince George runs the election and has the final word on dates, voting places, ID, and registration. Check the official sources:

  • City of Prince George — Elections ↗
  • Elections BC — Local elections ↗

Heads up: the steps above are the general British Columbia local-election rules and may be updated for 2026. Always confirm the specifics with the City of Prince George before you rely on them.
